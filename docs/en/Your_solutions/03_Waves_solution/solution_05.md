### Question 5: Echo Ranging

**1. Identify Given Variables:**
* Total time for echo ($t_{total}$): $1 \text{ s}$
* Speed of sound in air ($v$): $343 \text{ m/s}$
* Goal: Find the distance to the cliff ($d$).

**2. Physical Principle:**
An echo occurs when sound travels to a surface and reflects back to the source. This means the sound travels the distance to the cliff **twice**:
* Distance 1: From the person to the cliff.
* Distance 2: From the cliff back to the person.
* Total distance ($D_{total}$) = $2 \times d$

The basic formula for constant speed is:
$$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$$

---

**Step-by-Step Calculation:**

* **Step 1: Calculate the total distance traveled by the sound**
    Using the total time of $1 \text{ s}$:
    $$D_{total} = v \cdot t_{total}$$
    $$D_{total} = 343 \text{ m/s} \cdot 1 \text{ s}$$
    $$D_{total} = 343 \text{ m}$$

* **Step 2: Determine the distance to the cliff ($d$)**
    Since the sound traveled to the cliff and back, we must divide the total distance by 2:
    $$d = \frac{D_{total}}{2}$$
    $$d = \frac{343 \text{ m}}{2}$$

* **Step 3: Alternative Method (Time-based)**
    Alternatively, you can find the time it takes for the sound to reach the cliff (one way):
    $$t_{one-way} = \frac{t_{total}}{2} = \frac{1 \text{ s}}{2} = 0.5 \text{ s}$$
    Then calculate the distance:
    $$d = 343 \text{ m/s} \times 0.5 \text{ s} = 171.5 \text{ m}$$

---

**Final Result:**
The cliff is **$171.5 \text{ meters}$** away.
