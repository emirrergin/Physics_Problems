### Question 2: String Harmonics (Detailed Breakdown)

**1. Identify Given Variables:**
* Length of the string ($L$): $64 \text{ cm}$
* Fundamental frequency ($f_1$): $330 \text{ Hz}$
* Harmonic state: Fundamental frequency (1 antinode)

**2. Unit Conversion:**
Standard physics calculations use the SI unit system. We must convert centimeters to meters.
* $L = 64 \text{ cm} = 0.64 \text{ m}$

**3. Physical Principle (Standing Waves on a String):**
For a string fixed at both ends, the fundamental frequency (1st harmonic) creates a standing wave with one "loop" or antinode. 
* In this state, the length of the string ($L$) is equal to exactly **half of a wavelength** ($\frac{1}{2}\lambda$).
* Relationship: $L = \frac{\lambda}{2} \implies \lambda = 2L$

---

**Step-by-Step Calculation:**

* **Step 1: Calculate the Wavelength ($\lambda$)**
    Using the relationship for the fundamental frequency:
    $$\lambda = 2 \cdot L$$
    $$\lambda = 2 \cdot 0.64 \text{ m}$$
    $$\lambda = 1.28 \text{ m}$$

* **Step 2: Use the Wave Equation to find Speed ($v$)**
    The speed of the wave is the product of its frequency and wavelength:
    $$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

* **Step 3: Set up the calculation with units**
    $$v = 330 \text{ Hz} \cdot 1.28 \text{ m}$$
    Recall that $1 \text{ Hz} = \frac{1}{\text{s}}$, so the units become:
    $$v = 330 \cdot \frac{1}{\text{s}} \cdot 1.28 \text{ m} = 422.4 \text{ m/s}$$

---

**Final Result:**
The speed of the wave on this string is **$422.4 \text{ m/s}$**.
