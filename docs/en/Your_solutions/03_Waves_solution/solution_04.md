### Question 4: Phase Difference (Detailed Breakdown)

**1. Identify Given Variables:**
* Path difference (distance between two points, $\Delta x$): $\frac{\lambda}{3}$
* Goal: Find the phase difference ($\Delta \phi$) in radians.

**2. Physical Principle:**
A full wavelength ($\lambda$) represents one complete cycle of a wave. In terms of circular motion and trigonometry, one complete cycle corresponds to an angle of $2\pi$ radians (or $360^\circ$).

Therefore, the phase difference is directly proportional to the path difference. We can use a simple ratio or a formula.

**3. Formula Derivation:**
The relationship between phase difference ($\Delta \phi$) and path difference ($\Delta x$) is:
$$\frac{\Delta \phi}{2\pi} = \frac{\Delta x}{\lambda}$$

Rearranging for $\Delta \phi$:
$$\Delta \phi = \left( \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \right) \cdot \Delta x$$
*(Note: $\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$ is also known as the wave number, $k$.)*

---

**Step-by-Step Calculation:**

* **Step 1: Substitute the given distance ($\Delta x$) into the formula**
    $$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \left( \frac{\lambda}{3} \right)$$

* **Step 2: Simplify the expression**
    The wavelength ($\lambda$) terms in the numerator and denominator cancel each other out:
    $$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi \cdot \lambda}{3 \cdot \lambda}$$
    $$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}$$

* **Step 3: Unit Verification**
    The result is already in radians as requested. To visualize this in degrees (optional check):
    $$\frac{2 \cdot 180^\circ}{3} = 120^\circ$$

---

**Final Result:**
The phase difference between the two points is **$\frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ radians}$**.
