### Question 6: Wave Equation

**1. Identify the Standard Wave Equation:**
The general form of a traveling wave equation is:
$$y(x, t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$$

Where:
* $A$ = Amplitude
* $k$ = Wave number ($k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$)
* $\omega$ = Angular frequency ($\omega = 2\pi f$)

**2. Compare the Given Equation:**
The given equation is:
$$y(x, t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$$

By comparing the two, we can identify:
* $A = 0.05$
* $k = 2\pi$
* $\omega = 50\pi$

---

**Step-by-Step Solutions:**

**a) Amplitude ($A$)**
By direct observation from the equation:
$$\mathbf{A = 0.05 \text{ m}}$$

---

**b) Wavelength ($\lambda$)**
Using the wave number ($k$):
* **Formula:** $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \implies \lambda = \frac{2\pi}{k}$
* **Calculation:**
  $$\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi}$$
  $$\mathbf{\lambda = 1 \text{ m}}$$

---

**c) Frequency ($f$)**
Using the angular frequency ($\omega$):
* **Formula:** $\omega = 2\pi f \implies f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}$
* **Calculation:**
  $$f = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi}$$
  $$\mathbf{f = 25 \text{ Hz}}$$

---

**d) Wave Speed ($v$)**
There are two ways to calculate this:

* **Method 1 (Basic Wave Equation):**
    $$v = f \cdot \lambda$$
    $$v = 25 \text{ Hz} \cdot 1 \text{ m} = 25 \text{ m/s}$$

* **Method 2 (Using $k$ and $\omega$):**
    $$v = \frac{\omega}{k}$$
    $$v = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \text{ m/s}$$

**Final Result:**
$$\mathbf{v = 25 \text{ m/s}}$$
