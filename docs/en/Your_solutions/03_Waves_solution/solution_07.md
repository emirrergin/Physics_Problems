### Question 7: Standing Wave Modes 

**1. Identification of Given Physical Parameters:**
* **String Length ($L$):** The total physical distance between the two fixed ends is given as $80 \text{ cm}$.
* **Mode of Vibration ($n$):** The problem states there are **four antinodes**. In the context of a string fixed at both ends, the number of antinodes corresponds directly to the harmonic number ($n = 4$).
* **Target Variable:** Wavelength ($\lambda$).

**2. Mandatory Unit Conversion (SI Standard):**
Before performing any algebraic calculations, we must ensure all units are in the International System of Units (SI).
* Calculation: $L = 80 \text{ cm} \times \left( \frac{1 \text{ m}}{100 \text{ cm}} \right)$
* Result: **$L = 0.8 \text{ m}$**

**3. Theoretical Framework & Principle:**
On a string fixed at both ends, standing waves are formed by the interference of two traveling waves. A single "loop" or "envelope" represents half of a full wavelength ($\lambda/2$). Since there are 4 antinodes, there are 4 such half-wavelength loops fitting exactly into the length $L$.
* The fundamental geometric relationship is:
$$L = n \cdot \left( \frac{\lambda}{2} \right)$$

**4. Algebraic Manipulation (Isolating the Unknown):**
To find the wavelength ($\lambda$), we must isolate it on one side of the equation:
* Start with: $L = \frac{n \cdot \lambda}{2}$
* Multiply both sides by 2: $2L = n \cdot \lambda$
* Divide both sides by $n$:
$$\lambda = \frac{2 \cdot L}{n}$$

**5. Step-by-Step Numerical Substitution:**
* Substitute $L = 0.8 \text{ m}$ and $n = 4$ into the isolated formula:
$$\lambda = \frac{2 \cdot (0.8 \text{ m})}{4}$$

**6. Intermediate Arithmetic and Unit Analysis:**
* **Step A (Numerator Calculation):** Multiply the constants in the numerator.
  $$2 \cdot 0.8 \text{ m} = 1.6 \text{ m}$$
* **Step B (Final Division):** Divide the result by the number of antinodes ($n=4$).
  $$\lambda = \frac{1.6 \text{ m}}{4}$$
* **Step C (Unit Verification):** Since $n$ (the number of antinodes) is a dimensionless count, the unit of the result remains in meters ($\text{m}$), which is the correct unit for wavelength.
  $$\lambda = 0.4 \text{ m}$$

**7. Conversion to Alternative Units (Optional but Comprehensive):**
* In centimeters: $\lambda = 0.4 \text{ m} \times 100 \text{ cm/m} = 40 \text{ cm}$

---

**Final Result:**
The wavelength of the standing wave is exactly **$0.4 \text{ m}$** (or **$40 \text{ cm}$**).
