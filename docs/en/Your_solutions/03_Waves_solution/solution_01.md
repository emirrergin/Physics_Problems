### Question 1: Wave Properties

**1. Identify Given Variables:**
* Frequency of the source ($f$): $440 \text{ Hz}$ (This means $440$ cycles per second)
* Speed of sound in air ($v_{air}$): $343 \text{ m/s}$
* Speed of sound in water ($v_{water}$): $1482 \text{ m/s}$

**2. Physical Principle:**
The speed of a wave is the product of its frequency and its wavelength. This is expressed by the fundamental wave equation:
$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

To find the wavelength ($\lambda$), we rearrange the formula:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

---

**Part A: Calculation for Air**

* **Step 1: Set up the equation with units**
    $$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ Hz}}$$

* **Step 2: Understand the units (Unit Analysis)**
    Since $1 \text{ Hz} = \frac{1}{\text{s}}$, the calculation looks like this:
    $$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343 \text{ m}}{\text{s}} \cdot \frac{\text{s}}{440}$$
    The seconds ($\text{s}$) cancel out, leaving us with meters ($\text{m}$).

* **Step 3: Final Division**
    $$\lambda_{air} = 0.779545... \text{ m}$$
    **Result:** $\mathbf{\lambda_{air} \approx 0.78 \text{ m}}$

---

**Part B: Calculation for Water**

* **Step 1: Identify the constant**
    When the sound wave moves from air to water, the **frequency ($f$) stays the same ($440 \text{ Hz}$)** because it is determined by the vibrating source. Only the speed and wavelength change.

* **Step 2: Set up the equation**
    $$\lambda_{water} = \frac{v_{water}}{f}$$
    $$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ Hz}}$$

* **Step 3: Final Division**
    $$\lambda_{water} = 3.368181... \text{ m}$$
    **Result:** $\mathbf{\lambda_{water} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}}$
