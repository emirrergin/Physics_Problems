# ⚡ Physics Basics: Instantaneous Current Analysis

## 1. Problem Statement
the total charge ($Q$) flowing through a wire is expressed as a function of time ($t$):
$$Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5$$

**Task:** Calculate the instantaneous current ($I$) at exactly **$t = 3$ seconds**.

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principles

### The Definition of Current
Electric current is defined as the rate at which charge flows through a cross-section of a conductor. When the charge flow is non-uniform (varies with time), we calculate the **instantaneous current** by taking the first derivative of the charge function with respect to time:



$$I(t) = \frac{dQ(t)}{dt}$$

Where:
* **$I(t)$**: Instantaneous current (Amperes, A)
* **$Q(t)$**: Total charge (Coulombs, C)
* **$t$**: Time (Seconds, s)

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Differentiate the Charge Function
To find the general expression for current, we apply the power rule of differentiation to the given function $Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5$:
$$\frac{d}{dt}(5t^2) = 10t$$
$$\frac{d}{dt}(5) = 0$$

So, the current function is:
**$$I(t) = 10t$$**

### Step 2: Calculate Current at $t = 3$ s
Now, we substitute the specific time value ($t = 3$) into our derivative:
$$I(3) = 10 \times 3$$
**$I = 30 \, \text{A}$**

---

## 4. Final Result
The instantaneous current flowing through the wire at $t = 3$ seconds is **$30 \, \text{Amperes}$**.
