# 🔋 Physics Basics: Capacitors in Parallel

## 1. Problem Statement
Based on **image_1cae7a.png**, we have two capacitors connected in a parallel configuration:
*   **Capacitor 1 ($C_1$):** $4 \, \mu\text{F}$
*   **Capacitor 2 ($C_2$):** $6 \, \mu\text{F}$
*   **Battery Voltage ($V$):** $10 \, \text{V}$

**Tasks:**
1. Calculate the **total charge** ($Q_{total}$) stored on the capacitors.
2. Calculate the **total energy** ($U_{total}$) stored in the system.

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principles

### A. Equivalent Capacitance in Parallel
When capacitors are connected in parallel, they all experience the same voltage ($V$). The total or equivalent capacitance ($C_{eq}$) is simply the sum of the individual capacitances:
$$C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2 + ... + C_n$$


### B. Charge and Energy Formulas
*   **Charge ($Q$):** The charge stored is the product of capacitance and voltage.
    $$Q = C \cdot V$$
*   **Energy ($U$):** The electrical potential energy stored in a capacitor is given by:
    $$U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2$$


---

## 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Calculate Equivalent Capacitance ($C_{eq}$)
Since they are in parallel:
$$C_{eq} = 4 \, \mu\text{F} + 6 \, \mu\text{F}$$
**$C_{eq} = 10 \, \mu\text{F}$** (or $10 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{F}$)

### Step 2: Calculate Total Charge ($Q_{total}$)
Using the equivalent capacitance and the battery voltage:
$$Q_{total} = C_{eq} \cdot V$$
$$Q_{total} = (10 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{F}) \cdot 10 \, \text{V}$$
**$Q_{total} = 100 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{C}$** (or **$100 \, \mu\text{C}$**)

### Step 3: Calculate Total Energy Stored ($U_{total}$)
Using the energy formula:
$$U_{total} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot C_{eq} \cdot V^2$$
$$U_{total} = 0.5 \cdot (10 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{F}) \cdot (10 \, \text{V})^2$$
$$U_{total} = 0.5 \cdot 10^{-5} \cdot 100$$
**$U_{total} = 500 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{J}$** (or **$500 \, \mu\text{J}$**)
