# ⚡ Physics Basics: Power & Energy Analysis

## 1. Problem Statement
We are analyzing the electrical behavior of a single resistor:
*   **Resistance ($R$):** $100\,\Omega$
*   **Voltage ($V$):** $50\,\text{V}$
*   **Time ($t$):** $5\,\text{minutes}$

**Tasks:**
1. Calculate the **power dissipated** ($P$) by the resistor.
2. Calculate the **total energy consumed** ($E$) in 5 minutes.

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principles



### A. Electric Power ($P$)
Power is the rate at which electrical energy is converted into another form (usually heat in a resistor). When voltage and resistance are known, we use:
$$P = \frac{V^2}{R}$$

### B. Electric Energy ($E$)
Energy is the total amount of work done or heat dissipated over a specific time interval:
$$E = P \cdot t$$

*   **Note on Units:** Power is measured in **Watts (W)**. For energy to be in **Joules (J)**, time must be converted to **seconds (s)**.

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Calculate Power Dissipated ($P$)
Substitute the given values into the formula:
$$P = \frac{(50\,\text{V})^2}{100\,\Omega}$$
$$P = \frac{2500}{100}$$
**$P = 25\,\text{W}$**

### Step 2: Convert Time to Seconds
$$t = 5\,\text{minutes} \times 60\,\text{s/minute}$$
**$t = 300\,\text{s}$**

### Step 3: Calculate Energy Consumed ($E$)
Multiply the calculated power by the time in seconds:
$$E = 25\,\text{W} \times 300\,\text{s}$$
**$E = 7500\,\text{J}$** (or $7.5\,\text{kJ}$)

---

## 4. Final Results Summary
| Parameter | Symbol | Value | Unit |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **Power Dissipated** | $P$ | $25$ | $\text{W}$ |
| **Energy Consumed** | $E$ | $7500$ | $\text{J}$ |
