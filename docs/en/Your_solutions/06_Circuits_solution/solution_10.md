# ⚡ Physics Basics: Average Current Analysis (Lightning Bolt)

## 1. Problem Statement
we are calculating the electrical current generated by a natural phenomenon:
*   **Total Charge Transferred ($Q$):** $30 \, \text{C}$ (Coulombs)
*   **Time Duration ($\Delta t$):** $2 \, \text{ms}$ (milliseconds)

**Task:** Determine the **average current** ($I_{avg}$) of the lightning bolt.

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principles

### Definition of Electric Current
Electric current is defined as the rate of flow of electric charge through a surface per unit of time. The formula for average current is:

$$I_{avg} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}$$

Where:
*   **$I$**: Current measured in **Amperes (A)**.
*   **$Q$**: Charge measured in **Coulombs (C)**.
*   **$t$**: Time measured in **Seconds (s)**.

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Unit Conversion
In physics, we must use SI units for consistency. We need to convert milliseconds to seconds:
$$1 \, \text{ms} = 10^{-3} \, \text{s}$$
$$2 \, \text{ms} = 2 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{s} = 0.002 \, \text{s}$$

### Step 2: Apply the Formula
Substitute the given values into the current equation:
$$I_{avg} = \frac{30 \, \text{C}}{0.002 \, \text{s}}$$

### Step 3: Solve
$$I_{avg} = \frac{30000}{2}$$
**$I_{avg} = 15,000 \, \text{A}$** (or $15 \, \text{kA}$)

---

## 4. Final Result Summary
| Parameter | Symbol | Value | Unit |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **Total Charge** | $Q$ | $30$ | $\text{C}$ |
| **Time Interval** | $\Delta t$ | $0.002$ | $\text{s}$ |
| **Average Current** | $I_{avg}$ | **$15,000$** | **$\text{A}$** |
