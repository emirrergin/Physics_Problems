# ⚡ Physics Basics: Transformer Turns Ratio Analysis

## 1. Problem Statement
we are calculating the required turns for a step-down transformer:
*   **Primary Voltage ($V_p$):** $120 \, \text{V AC}$
*   **Secondary Voltage ($V_s$):** $9.0 \, \text{V AC}$
*   **Primary Coil Turns ($N_p$):** $400 \, \text{turns}$

**Task:** Determine the number of turns required for the **secondary coil** ($N_s$).

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principle
The operation of a transformer is governed by Faraday's Law of Induction. For an ideal transformer, the ratio of the voltages is directly proportional to the ratio of the number of turns in the coils:

$$\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$



Where:
*   **$V_p, V_s$**: Voltages of the primary and secondary coils.
*   **$N_p, N_s$**: Number of turns in the primary and secondary coils.

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Rearrange the Formula
To solve for the secondary turns ($N_s$), we rearrange the equation:
$$N_s = N_p \times \frac{V_s}{V_p}$$

### Step 2: Substitute the Known Values
Plug in the values provided in the problem:
$$N_s = 400 \times \frac{9.0 \, \text{V}}{120 \, \text{V}}$$

### Step 3: Perform the Math
$$N_s = 400 \times 0.075$$
**$N_s = 30 \, \text{turns}$**

---
