# Physics Basics: Force Comparison in a Hydrogen Atom

## 1. Problem Description
Calculate the magnitude of the **Electric Force ($F_e$)** and the **Gravitational Force ($F_g$)** between an electron and a proton in a hydrogen atom, given an average distance of $r \approx 5.3 \times 10^{-11}$ m. Then, determine the ratio $F_e/F_g$.

## 2. Constants & Given Data
To solve this, we need the following fundamental physical constants:
* **Distance ($r$):** $5.3 \times 10^{-11}$ m
* **Charge of electron/proton ($e$):** $1.60 \times 10^{-19}$ C
* **Mass of proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27}$ kg
* **Mass of electron ($m_e$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg
* **Coulomb Constant ($k$):** $8.99 \times 10^9$ N·m²/C²
* **Gravitational Constant ($G$):** $6.67 \times 10^{-11}$ N·m²/kg²

## 3. Physical Principles
We use two fundamental laws of physics:
1. **Coulomb's Law (Electric Force):** $$F_e = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$
2. **Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation (Gravitational Force):** $$F_g = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

## 4. Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Calculate the Electric Force ($F_e$)
$$F_e = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{(1.60 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot (1.60 \times 10^{-19})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_e = \frac{2.301 \times 10^{-28}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}$$
**$$F_e \approx 8.19 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$$**

### Step 2: Calculate the Gravitational Force ($F_g$)
$$F_g = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27}) \cdot (9.11 \times 10^{-31})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_g = \frac{1.015 \times 10^{-67}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}$$
**$$F_g \approx 3.61 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$$**

### Step 3: Calculate the Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)
Notice that since $r^2$ is in the denominator of both formulas, it cancels out when we take the ratio:
$$\frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{k \cdot e^2}{G \cdot m_p \cdot m_e}$$
$$\frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{8.19 \times 10^{-8}}{3.61 \times 10^{-47}}$$
**$$\frac{F_e}{F_g} \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}$$**

## 5. Conclusion
The ratio is approximately **$2.27 \times 10^{39}$**. 

This staggering result shows that the electric force is about **2.27 undecillion times stronger** than the gravitational force at the atomic level. This is why gravity is considered negligible when studying the interactions of subatomic particles.
