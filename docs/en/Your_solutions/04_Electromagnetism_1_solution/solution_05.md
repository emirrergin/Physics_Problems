# Physics Basics: Field Levitation (Proton)

## 1. Problem Description
What electric field strength ($E$) is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface? 

## 2. Given Data
* **Mass of proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27}$ kg
* **Charge of proton ($e$):** $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C
* **Gravitational acceleration ($g$):** $9.8$ m/s²
* **Goal:** Find the Electric Field Strength ($E$)

## 3. Physical Principles
For the proton to levitate (stay in static equilibrium), the net force acting on it must be zero ($\sum F = 0$). This means the upward **Electric Force ($F_e$)** must perfectly balance the downward **Gravitational Force ($F_g$)**.

$$F_e = F_g$$

**Key Formulas:**
* **Gravitational Force (Weight):** $F_g = m \cdot g$
* **Electric Force:** $F_e = q \cdot E$

## 4. Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Establishing Equilibrium
We equate the two forces acting on the proton:
$$q \cdot E = m \cdot g$$

### Step 2: Isolating the Variable $E$
To find the electric field strength, we rearrange the formula to solve for $E$:
$$E = \frac{m \cdot g}{q}$$

### Step 3: Substituting the Values
Plug in the constants provided in the problem:
$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \cdot (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$

### Step 4: Final Calculation
First, let's find the magnitude of the gravitational force:
$$F_g \approx 1.6366 \times 10^{-26} \text{ N}$$

Now, divide by the charge of the proton:
$$E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$E \approx 1.022875 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$

## 5. Conclusion
The required electric field strength to levitate a proton is approximately:
**$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$**

> **Directional Note:** Since the proton has a positive charge, the electric force follows the direction of the electric field. Therefore, to counteract gravity, the electric field must be directed **upward**.
