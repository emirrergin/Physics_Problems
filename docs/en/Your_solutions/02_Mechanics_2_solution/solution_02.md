## Harmonic Motion Analysis

**Problem Statement:**
A $10 \text{ kg}$ mass is attached to a spring and oscillates according to the equation $x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)$ (in meters). 
1. What is the spring constant $k$? 
2. What is the total mechanical energy of the system?

---

### Step 1: Identify Given Information
From the problem description and the standard equation for simple harmonic motion $x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)$, we can identify the following parameters:
* **Mass ($m$):** $10 \text{ kg}$
* **Amplitude ($A$):** $0.2 \text{ m}$
* **Angular frequency ($\omega$):** $10\pi \text{ rad/s}$

---

### Step 2: Calculate the Spring Constant ($k$)

**Formula:**
The angular frequency $\omega$ is related to the spring constant $k$ and the mass $m$ by the following formula:
$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$

**Calculation:**
1. Square both sides to isolate $k$:
$$\omega^2 = \frac{k}{m}$$
$$k = m \cdot \omega^2$$

2. Substitute the known values:
$$k = 10 \cdot (10\pi)^2$$
$$k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2$$
$$k = 1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$$

**Result:**
The spring constant $k$ is exactly **$1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$** (Approximately $9869.6 \text{ N/m}$).

---

### Step 3: Calculate the Total Mechanical Energy ($E$)

**Formula:**
The total mechanical energy of a simple harmonic oscillator is conserved and depends on the spring constant and the square of the amplitude:
$$E = \frac{1}{2} k A^2$$

**Calculation:**
1. Substitute the values we found and were given:
$$E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot (1000\pi^2) \cdot (0.2)^2$$

2. Perform the arithmetic:
$$E = 500\pi^2 \cdot 0.04$$
$$E = 20\pi^2 \text{ Joules}$$

**Result:**
The total mechanical energy of the system is exactly **$20\pi^2 \text{ J}$** (Approximately $197.4 \text{ J}$).
