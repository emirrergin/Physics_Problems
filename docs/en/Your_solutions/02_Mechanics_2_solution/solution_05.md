## Inelastic Collision Analysis

**Problem Statement:**
A $70 \text{ kg}$ runner moving at $3 \text{ m/s}$ jumps onto a $140 \text{ kg}$ stationary cart. What is the final speed of the cart with the runner? Is kinetic energy conserved in this collision? Explain.

---

### Step 1: Identify Given Information
* **Mass of runner ($m_1$):** $70 \text{ kg}$
* **Initial velocity of runner ($v_1$):** $3 \text{ m/s}$
* **Mass of cart ($m_2$):** $140 \text{ kg}$
* **Initial velocity of cart ($v_2$):** $0 \text{ m/s}$ (stationary)
* **Collision type:** Perfectly inelastic (the runner and cart move together after impact).

---

### Step 2: Calculate Final Speed (Conservation of Momentum)
In the absence of external forces, the total momentum of an isolated system is always conserved, regardless of the type of collision.

**Formula:**
$$p_{initial} = p_{final}$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

**Calculation:**
1. Substitute the known values into the equation:
$$70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0 = (70 + 140) \cdot v_f$$
$$210 + 0 = 210 \cdot v_f$$

2. Isolate the final velocity ($v_f$):
$$210 = 210 \cdot v_f$$
$$v_f = \frac{210}{210}$$
$$v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}$$

**Result (Part 1):**
The final speed of the cart with the runner is exactly **$1 \text{ m/s}$**.

---

### Step 3: Analyze Kinetic Energy Conservation
To determine if kinetic energy is conserved, we must calculate the total kinetic energy of the system before and after the collision. 
**Formula:** $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$

**Initial Kinetic Energy ($KE_{initial}$):**
$$KE_{initial} = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2v_2^2$$
$$KE_{initial} = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3)^2 + 0$$
$$KE_{initial} = 35 \cdot 9$$
$$KE_{initial} = 315 \text{ J}$$

**Final Kinetic Energy ($KE_{final}$):**
$$KE_{final} = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2)v_f^2$$
$$KE_{final} = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1)^2$$
$$KE_{final} = 105 \cdot 1$$
$$KE_{final} = 105 \text{ J}$$

**Result (Part 2) & Explanation:**
**No**, kinetic energy is *not* conserved. The system started with $315 \text{ J}$ of kinetic energy and ended with only $105 \text{ J}$. 

In a perfectly inelastic collision (where objects stick together), kinetic energy is never conserved. The "missing" energy ($315 \text{ J} - 105 \text{ J} = 210 \text{ J}$) is not destroyed; rather, it is transformed into other non-recoverable forms of energy. In this scenario, it is primarily dissipated as thermal energy (friction between the runner's shoes and the cart's surface upon impact) and sound energy.
