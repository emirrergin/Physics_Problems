## Energy Dissipation Analysis

**Problem Statement:**
A tennis ball is dropped from a height of 2.0 m. After each bounce, it loses 30% of its mechanical energy. To what height does it rise after the second bounce?

---

### Step 1: Identify Given Information and Principles
* **Initial height ($h_0$):** 2.0 m
* **Energy lost per bounce:** 30%
* **Energy retained per bounce:** 100% - 30% = 70% (or a factor of 0.7)
* **Principle:** Gravitational potential energy is calculated as $PE = mgh$. Since the mass ($m$) of the ball and the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) remain constant, the height ($h$) the ball reaches is directly proportional to its mechanical energy. Therefore, losing 30% of its energy means it will only reach 70% of its previous height.

---

### Step 2: Calculate Height After the First Bounce ($h_1$)
After the first bounce, the ball retains 70% of its initial energy, so it reaches 70% of its initial height.

$$h_1 = 0.70 \cdot h_0$$
$$h_1 = 0.70 \cdot 2.0$$
$$h_1 = 1.4 \text{ m}$$

---

### Step 3: Calculate Height After the Second Bounce ($h_2$)
After the second bounce, the ball retains 70% of the energy it had *after the first bounce*, meaning it reaches 70% of $h_1$.

$$h_2 = 0.70 \cdot h_1$$
$$h_2 = 0.70 \cdot 1.4$$
$$h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}$$

*(Alternatively, you can calculate this in one step: $h_2 = h_0 \cdot (0.70)^2 = 2.0 \cdot 0.49 = 0.98 \text{ m}$)*

**Result:**
The tennis ball rises to a height of **0.98 m** (or **98 cm**) after the second bounce.
