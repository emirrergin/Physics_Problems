## Energy & Momentum Analysis

**Problem Statement:**
A $0.5 \text{ kg}$ block slides down a frictionless track from a height of $3.0 \text{ m}$. At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a $1.5 \text{ kg}$ block, which is initially at rest. What is the speed of the combined mass just after the collision?

---

### Step 1: Identify Given Information
* **Mass of sliding block ($m_1$):** $0.5 \text{ kg}$
* **Initial height of sliding block ($h$):** $3.0 \text{ m}$
* **Mass of resting block ($m_2$):** $1.5 \text{ kg}$
* **Acceleration due to gravity ($g$):** $\approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
* **Collision type:** Perfectly inelastic (the blocks stick together).

---

### Step 2: Conservation of Energy (Finding speed before collision)
First, we need to determine the speed of the first block ($m_1$) exactly at the bottom of the track, just before it hits the second block. Because the track is frictionless, we can use the conservation of mechanical energy. Its gravitational potential energy at the top is completely converted into kinetic energy at the bottom.

$$PE_{top} = KE_{bottom}$$
$$m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$$

The mass $m_1$ cancels out from both sides:
$$gh = \frac{1}{2}v_1^2$$

Isolate the velocity ($v_1$):
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$

Substitute the values into the equation:
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3.0}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.8}$$
$$v_1 \approx 7.668 \text{ m/s}$$

*(This is the velocity of the $0.5 \text{ kg}$ block just before impact).*

---

### Step 3: Conservation of Momentum (The Collision)
In any collision, total momentum is conserved. Because the two blocks stick together after the impact, this is a perfectly inelastic collision. They will share a single final velocity ($v_f$).

**Formula:**
$$p_{initial} = p_{final}$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

Since the second block ($1.5 \text{ kg}$) is initially at rest, its initial velocity $v_2 = 0$:
$$m_1v_1 + 0 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

Substitute the known values into the equation:
$$0.5 \cdot 7.668 = (0.5 + 1.5) \cdot v_f$$
$$3.834 = 2.0 \cdot v_f$$

Now, isolate the final velocity ($v_f$):
$$v_f = \frac{3.834}{2.0}$$
$$v_f = 1.917 \text{ m/s}$$

**Result:**
The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is approximately **$1.92 \text{ m/s}$**.
