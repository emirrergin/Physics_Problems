## 8. Circular Motion — Solution

**Given:**
- Earth's radius: $R = 6378 \text{ km} = 6{,}378{,}000 \text{ m}$
- Earth's rotation period: $T = 24 \text{ hours} = 86{,}400 \text{ s}$

---

### Step 1: Find the Speed of a Person at the Equator

A person at the equator travels the full circumference of Earth in one day:

$$v = \frac{2\pi R}{T} = \frac{2\pi \times 6{,}378{,}000}{86{,}400}$$

$$v = \frac{40{,}074{,}156}{86{,}400} \approx 463.8 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Step 2: Apply the Centripetal Acceleration Formula

$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{R}$$

$$a_c = \frac{(463.8)^2}{6{,}378{,}000} = \frac{215{,}110}{6{,}378{,}000}$$

$$\boxed{a_c \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2}$$

---

### Alternative Formula (directly using $T$)

$$a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 R}{T^2}$$

$$a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 \times 6{,}378{,}000}{(86{,}400)^2} = \frac{251{,}495{,}016}{7{,}464{,}960{,}000}$$

$$\boxed{a_c \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2}$$

Both methods give the same result. ✓

---

### Comparison with Gravitational Acceleration

$$\frac{a_c}{g} = \frac{0.0337}{9.81} \approx 0.0034$$

> The centripetal acceleration at the equator is only about **0.34% of $g$**.
> This is why we don't "fly off" the Earth — gravity is far stronger than
> the centripetal effect of Earth's rotation.

---

### Summary

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---|
| Equatorial speed | $v \approx 463.8$ m/s |
| Centripetal acceleration | $a_c \approx 0.0337$ m/s² |
| Compared to $g = 9.81$ m/s² | $\approx 0.34\%$ of $g$ |
