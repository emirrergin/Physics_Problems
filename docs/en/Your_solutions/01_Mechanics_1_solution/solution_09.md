## 9. Momentum Comparison — Solution

**Given:**
- Fly: $m_1 = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$, $v_1 = 10 \text{ m/s}$
- Tennis ball: $m_2 = 60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$, $v_2 = 1 \text{ m/s}$

---

### Momentum Formula

$$p = m \times v$$

---

### Momentum of the Fly

$$p_1 = 0.002 \times 10 = \boxed{0.02 \text{ kg m/s}}$$

---

### Momentum of the Tennis Ball

$$p_2 = 0.060 \times 1 = \boxed{0.06 \text{ kg m/s}}$$

---

### Comparison

$$\frac{p_2}{p_1} = \frac{0.06}{0.02} = 3$$

---

### Result

> The **tennis ball** has **3 times greater momentum** than the fly,
> despite moving much slower. This shows that **mass plays a
> dominant role** in momentum when the speed difference is not large enough
> to compensate.

| Object | Mass | Speed | Momentum |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fly | 2 g | 10 m/s | 0.02 kg m/s |
| Tennis ball | 60 g | 1 m/s | 0.06 kg m/s |

**Winner: Tennis ball** with 3x more momentum.
