## 5. Relative Velocity — Solution

**Given:**
- River flows East at: $v_{river} = 2$ m/s
- Boat speed in still water: $v_{boat} = 5$ m/s
- River width: $d = 200$ m
- Desired direction: directly **North**

---

### Step 1: Understand the Problem

The river pushes the boat East. To go **straight North**, the boat must
aim slightly **West** to cancel the river's effect.

$$\vec{v}_{boat} + \vec{v}_{river} = \vec{v}_{resultant}$$

We want the resultant to point **purely North** (no East/West component).

---

### Step 2: Find the Heading Angle

The boat must aim at angle $\theta$ west of north.
The horizontal (East-West) components must cancel:

$$v_{boat} \sin\theta = v_{river}$$

$$\sin\theta = \frac{v_{river}}{v_{boat}} = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$

$$\boxed{\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx 23.58° \text{ west of north}}$$

---

### Step 3: Find the Resultant Speed (Northward)

Using the Pythagorean theorem:

$$v_{north} = \sqrt{v_{boat}^2 - v_{river}^2}$$

$$v_{north} = \sqrt{5^2 - 2^2} = \sqrt{25 - 4} = \sqrt{21}$$

$$\boxed{v_{north} \approx 4.583 \text{ m/s}}$$

---

### Step 4: Find the Crossing Time

$$t = \frac{d}{v_{north}} = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}}$$

$$\boxed{t \approx 43.6 \text{ s}}$$

---

### Summary

| | Value |
|---|---|
| Heading angle | $23.58°$ west of north |
| Northward speed | $\sqrt{21} \approx 4.583$ m/s |
| Time to cross | $\approx 43.6$ s |

The boat heads **west of north at 23.58°** so that the river current
exactly cancels the westward component, resulting in a perfectly
straight path across the river.
