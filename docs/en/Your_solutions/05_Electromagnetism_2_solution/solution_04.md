# 🔄 Physics Basics: Magnetic Torque on a Loop

## 1. Problem Statement
A rectangular loop of wire with dimensions **$10 \, \text{cm}$ by $5 \, \text{cm}$** carries a current of **$2 \, \text{A}$**. A uniform magnetic field of **$B = 0.3 \, \text{T}$** is applied **parallel to the plane of the loop**. What is the magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop?

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principle: Magnetic Torque
When a current-carrying loop is placed in a magnetic field, it experiences a torque ($\tau$) that tends to rotate the loop. The formula for torque is:

$$\tau = N \cdot I \cdot A \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
* **$\tau$ (Tau):** Torque (Unit: $N \cdot m$)
* **$N$:** Number of turns (in this case, $N = 1$)
* **$I$:** Current in Amperes ($A$)
* **$A$:** Area of the loop ($m^2$)
* **$B$:** Magnetic field strength (Tesla, $T$)
* **$\theta$:** The angle between the **magnetic field** and the **normal vector** (perpendicular) to the plane of the loop.


---

## 3. Given Information
* **Dimensions:** $10 \, \text{cm} \times 5 \, \text{cm} \rightarrow 0.1 \, \text{m} \times 0.05 \, \text{m}$
* **Current ($I$):** $2 \, \text{A}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.3 \, \text{T}$
* **Orientation:** Parallel to the plane.

---

## 4. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Calculate the Area ($A$)
Area must be in square meters ($m^2$):
$$A = 0.1 \, \text{m} \times 0.05 \, \text{m} = 0.005 \, \text{m}^2$$

### Step 2: Determine the Angle ($\theta$)
This is the most critical step. 
* The problem states the field is **parallel** to the plane of the loop.
* However, $\theta$ is the angle between the field and the **normal** (perpendicular line) of the loop.
* If the field is parallel to the surface, it is **perpendicular ($90^\circ$)** to the normal vector.
$$\theta = 90^\circ \implies \sin(90^\circ) = 1$$

> *Note: This means the torque is at its maximum value.*

### Step 3: Substitute into the Formula
$$\tau = 1 \cdot 2 \, \text{A} \cdot 0.005 \, \text{m}^2 \cdot 0.3 \, \text{T} \cdot 1$$

### Step 4: Final Multiplication
$$\tau = 2 \cdot 0.005 \cdot 0.3$$
$$\tau = 0.01 \cdot 0.3$$
$$\tau = 0.003 \, \text{N} \cdot \text{m}$$

---

## 5. Final Result
The magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop is **$0.003 \, \text{N} \cdot \text{m}$** (or $3 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{N} \cdot \text{m}$).
