# 🔦 Physics Basics: Refraction (Snell's Law)

## 1. Problem Statement
A light ray travels from **air ($n = 1.00$)** into **glass ($n = 1.50$)**. If the **angle of incidence is $30^\circ$**, what is the **angle of refraction**?

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principle: Snell's Law
Snell's Law describes the relationship between the angles of incidence and refraction when light passes through the boundary between two different isotropic media (like air and glass).


### The Formula:
$$n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)$$

Where:
* **$n_1$**: Refractive index of the first medium (Air)
* **$\theta_1$**: Angle of incidence (angle in air)
* **$n_2$**: Refractive index of the second medium (Glass)
* **$\theta_2$**: Angle of refraction (angle in glass)

### The "Logic" of Bending:
When light moves from a **lower** refractive index (faster medium) to a **higher** refractive index (slower medium), it **bends toward the normal**. This means we expect our final angle ($\theta_2$) to be smaller than $30^\circ$.


---

## 3. Given Information
* **$n_1$ (Air):** $1.00$
* **$n_2$ (Glass):** $1.50$
* **Angle of Incidence ($\theta_1$):** $30^\circ$
* **$\sin(30^\circ)$:** $0.5$

---

## 4. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Set up the equation
$$1.00 \cdot \sin(30^\circ) = 1.50 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)$$

### Step 2: Plug in the known sine value
$$1.00 \cdot 0.5 = 1.50 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)$$
$$0.5 = 1.50 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)$$

### Step 3: Solve for $\sin(\theta_2)$
$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.50}$$
$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.3333$$

### Step 4: Calculate the angle ($\theta_2$)
To find the angle, we take the inverse sine ($\arcsin$):
$$\theta_2 = \arcsin(0.3333)$$
$$\theta_2 \approx 19.47^\circ$$

---

## 5. Final Result
The angle of refraction is approximately **$19.47^\circ$**.
