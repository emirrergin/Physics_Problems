# 📡 Physics Basics: The Electromagnetic Spectrum

## 1. Problem Statement
List the following types of electromagnetic radiation in order of **increasing wavelength**:
* Infrared
* Ultraviolet
* Microwaves
* X-rays
* Radio waves
* Gamma rays

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principle
Electromagnetic waves are classified by their **frequency ($f$)** and **wavelength ($\lambda$)**. There is an inverse relationship between the two:

$$c = f \cdot \lambda$$

* **High Energy/Frequency** = Short Wavelength (Gamma rays, X-rays)
* **Low Energy/Frequency** = Long Wavelength (Radio waves, Microwaves)


---

## 3. The Order of Increasing Wavelength
To list these from **shortest to longest** wavelength, we essentially list them from **highest to lowest** energy.

### 1. Gamma Rays
* **Shortest wavelength** (Less than $0.01$ nanometers).
* Highest energy and frequency. Used in cancer treatment and nuclear physics.

### 2. X-rays
* Longer than Gamma rays ($0.01 \, \text{nm}$ to $10 \, \text{nm}$).
* High energy, used for medical imaging.

### 3. Ultraviolet (UV)
* Between X-rays and Visible light ($10 \, \text{nm}$ to $400 \, \text{nm}$).
* Causes sunburns and is used for sterilization.

### 4. Infrared (IR)
* Longer than Visible light ($700 \, \text{nm}$ to $1 \, \text{mm}$).
* Perceived as heat. Used in remote controls and thermal cameras.

### 5. Microwaves
* Longer than Infrared ($1 \, \text{mm}$ to $1 \, \text{m}$).
* Used in cooking and radar technology.

### 6. Radio Waves
* **Longest wavelength** (From $1 \, \text{meter}$ to hundreds of kilometers).
* Lowest energy. Used for communication and broadcasting.

---

## 4. Final Ordered List (Shortest to Longest $\lambda$)
1.  **Gamma rays**
2.  **X-rays**
3.  **Ultraviolet**
4.  **Infrared**
5.  **Microwaves**
6.  **Radio waves**
