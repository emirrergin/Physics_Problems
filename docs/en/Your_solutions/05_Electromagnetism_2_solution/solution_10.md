# 💎 Physics Basics: Speed of Light in Media

## 1. Problem Statement
What is the speed of light in a **diamond**, which has an index of refraction **$n = 2.42$**?

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principle: Index of Refraction
The index of refraction ($n$) of a material is a dimensionless number that describes how fast light travels through that material relative to a vacuum.


### The Formula:
$$n = \frac{c}{v}$$

Which can be rearranged to find the velocity ($v$) in the medium:
$$v = \frac{c}{n}$$

Where:
* **$v$**: Speed of light in the medium (Diamond).
* **$c$**: Speed of light in a vacuum ($\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$).
* **$n$**: Index of refraction of the medium.

---

## 3. Given Information
* **Refractive Index ($n$):** $2.42$
* **Speed of Light in Vacuum ($c$):** $3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$

---

## 4. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Set up the formula
To find the speed in diamond, we divide the universal speed limit ($c$) by the material's refractive index ($n$).
$$v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{2.42}$$

### Step 2: Perform the division
$$3.00 \div 2.42 \approx 1.23966...$$

### Step 3: Scientific Notation
Rounding to three significant figures:
**$v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$**

---

## 5. Final Result
The speed of light in a diamond is approximately **$1.24 \times 10^8 \, \text{meters per second}$**.
