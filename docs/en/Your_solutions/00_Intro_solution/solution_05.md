## 5. Trigonometry — Solution

Given:

$$|\vec{A}| = 15, \quad \theta = 60°$$

---

### Formula

For any vector making an angle $\theta$ with the horizontal axis:

$$A_x = |\vec{A}| \cos\theta$$

$$A_y = |\vec{A}| \sin\theta$$

---

### Step 1: Horizontal Component $A_x$

$$A_x = 15 \cos(60°) = 15 \times \frac{1}{2}$$

$$\boxed{A_x = 7.5}$$

---

### Step 2: Vertical Component $A_y$

$$A_y = 15 \sin(60°) = 15 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$$

$$\boxed{A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99}$$

---

### Verification

Reconstruct the magnitude from components:

$$|\vec{A}| = \sqrt{A_x^2 + A_y^2} = \sqrt{7.5^2 + 12.99^2} = \sqrt{56.25 + 168.74} = \sqrt{225} = 15 \checkmark$$

---

### Result

$$\vec{A} = [A_x,\ A_y] = \left[7.5,\ \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}\right] \approx [7.50,\ 12.99]$$
