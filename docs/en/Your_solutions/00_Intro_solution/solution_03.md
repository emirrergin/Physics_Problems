## 3. Proportionality — Solution

The Universal Law of Gravitation:

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

---

### New Conditions

$$r' = 2r, \quad m_1' = \frac{m_1}{2}, \quad m_2' = \frac{m_2}{2}$$

---

### Step 1: Write the New Force $F'$

$$F' = G \frac{m_1' \cdot m_2'}{(r')^2}$$

Substitute the new values:

$$F' = G \frac{\dfrac{m_1}{2} \cdot \dfrac{m_2}{2}}{(2r)^2}$$

---

### Step 2: Simplify the Numerator and Denominator

$$F' = G \frac{\dfrac{m_1 m_2}{4}}{4r^2}$$

$$F' = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{16 r^2}$$

---

### Step 3: Compare $F'$ with the Original $F$

$$F' = \frac{1}{16} \cdot G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} = \frac{1}{16} F$$

---

### Result

$$\boxed{F' = \frac{F}{16}}$$

The gravitational force **decreases by a factor of 16** when the distance is doubled and both masses are halved.
