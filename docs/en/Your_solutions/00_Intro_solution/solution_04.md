## 4. Rearranging Formulas ‚Äî Solution

The period of a simple pendulum:

$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

---

### Step 1: Divide Both Sides by $2\pi$

$$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

---

### Step 2: Square Both Sides

$$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}$$

$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

---

### Step 3: Solve for $g$

$$g = \frac{L}{\dfrac{T^2}{4\pi^2}}$$

$$\boxed{g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}}$$

---

### Verification

Substitute $g = \dfrac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$ back into the original formula:

$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{\dfrac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}}} = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2}} = 2\pi \cdot \frac{T}{2\pi} = T \checkmark$$
